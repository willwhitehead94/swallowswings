Our four pillars

Meet the groups we work with most

Primary Education & Community Settings

Our shows are inspired by traditional African and Afro-Caribbean tales: youngsters benefit from finding threads common to all humanity. We travel to visit children at schools and in community groups bringing a safe place to explore, learn and engage with people from diverse backgrounds.

Neurodiversity


Puppetry is the perfect medium to engage many neurodiverse children. For children with different learning styles, the sensory immediacy of puppets and puppetry creates new channels for expression. Shadow puppetry workshops invite all abilities!

Isolated Elders


People living in assisted living centres or older people social clubs: our workshops provide entertainment, moments of joy and playfulness and are a launch pad for reminiscence therapy.

Refugees, Asylum Seekers & Migrants

Storytelling and puppet making are a rich and gentle means of processing, sharing and recovering from each journey. Sharing stories builds community.

Anansi & the Sweet Forgotten

Touring from October 2021
In memoradum of Allan Constantine Holson, "Sparky"

Using traditional West African stories about Anansi the Spider as a meeting place, we enchant, entertain, educate and build cross- generational dialogue. The story is told using puppets made from everyday materials, beautiful original music and spoken word poetry.

The shows are a springboard to learn about the journey of the Anansi stories from Africa to the UK via the Caribbean. This journey follows enslaved African people to the Carribean and then the migration of their descendants to the UK with the Windrush generation.

The show offers plenty of potential for further explorations of puppetry and story telling as well as the historical context of the stories.

Touring from October 2021. Book now!

Behind The Curtain

The people who make our magic happen

Caroline Ada

Puppeteer & Facilitator. After two decades as a primary school teacher & SEN educator, Caroline trained as a puppeteer at the Curious School of Puppetry and Little Angel Theatre. She is the creative heart of Swallow’s Wings.

Eileen Bellot

Director & Community Artist. Eileen’s work has been staged at Arcola Theatre, Vault Festival, Park Theatre, Ovalhouse, Albany Theatre and Canada Water Theatre. www.questlife.co.uk

Abstract Benna

Writer & Spoken Word Artist of Bajan & Jamaican heritage who has collaborated with major organisations including the Roundhouse, NHS and BBC 5 Live. www.abstractbenna.com

Mosi Conde

Musician & Composer. Award-winning Kora player who has performed at world-class venues including WOMAD, the Royal Albert Hall and the Royal Festival Hall. Recently featured live and interviewed on BBC Radio’s World on 3. www.greennote.co.uk/production/mosi-conde-kaira-kora-africa

Amber Rose Perry

Puppeteer & Actor. Puppeteer for the BBC TV series His Dark Materials and a commissioned show by the Museum of London. She is Artistic Director of Moth Physical Theatre and a Curious School of Puppetry alumni. www.mothphysicaltheatre.com
